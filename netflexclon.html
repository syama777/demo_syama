Yes, that's correct! The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio is calculated by dividing the Current Market Price (CMP) by Earnings Per Share (EPS). P/E Ratio = CMP / EPS Where: - P/E Ratio: A measure of how much investors are willing to pay for each rupee of earnings. - CMP (Current Market Price): The current price of the stock. - EPS (Earnings Per Share): The net profit earned by the company per share. For example: - CMP: ₹100 - EPS: ₹5 P/E Ratio = ₹100 / ₹5 = 20 This means investors are willing to pay ₹20 for every ₹1 of earnings. A higher P/E ratio indicates: - Higher growth expectations - Higher investor confidence - Possibly overvalued stock A lower P/E ratio indicates: - Lower growth expectations - Lower investor confidence - Possibly undervalued stock Keep in mind that P/E ratio varies across industries and sectors. It's essential to compare the P/E ratio with industry peers and historical averages to gain meaningful insights.
